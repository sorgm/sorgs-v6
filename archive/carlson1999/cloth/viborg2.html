Viborg

Excavations in 1981 and 1984/85 by Viborg stiftsmuseum have established that the town of Viborg, situated in the centre of Jutland, already existed in the Viking age, the dating being based upon dendrochronology and coins. Remains of houses, wells and fences were preserved, and the objects found indicate that a large number of different artisans had lived and worked here.
