Tunic

Kragelund


Pattern drawing based on Hald in Nockert

Found in the Kraglund Bog by Viborg in Denmark, it was found with a pair of shoes (now lost) but were of a pointed toe 12th-13th Century design. 

According to "Stella" hecaba@inspire.net.nz to 75years@yahoogroups 15 Feb 2002;  Porl Grinder-Hansen, curator Danish Middle Ages and Renaissance, Nat. Mus. of Denmark had this carbon14 dated in 1998 by the AMS-laboratory in ï¿½rhus, Jutland, using the accelerator technique and calibrated according to Stuiver and Pearson 1993.  It was dated to c.1040-1155.

There is a 7 cm (2.76") opening extending down from the neck opening.

The material of this outfit is a "threeshaft frieze".

  • Tunic Length: 114 cm (44.9")
  • Hem Circumference: 250 cm (98.4")
  • Neck Opening Circumference: 82 cm
  • Waist Circumference: 114 cm (44.9")
  • Arm Length: 63 cm (24.8")
  • Armhole Circumference: 75 cm (29.5")

  • Warp is 11 threads/cm (27.9 threads per inch)
  • Weft is 7 theads/cm (19.05 threads per inch)
